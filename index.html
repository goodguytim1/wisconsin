Understanding the Legal / Taxation Issue

Overview of the Concern:

  • Out-of-state partners provide essential support services for WI-based travel advisors.
  • Wisconsin wants to tax 100% of the gross commissions to these out-of-state partners.
  • Meanwhile, Wisconsin travel advisors already pay personal income tax on net commissions.

Because of this, the same commission is effectively taxed twice: once through the out-of-state partner’s gross payment and again when the WI advisor pays income tax.

Why is This Potentially Problematic?

  • Commerce Clause Concerns: States usually cannot unduly burden interstate commerce.
  • Double Taxation: Advisors can lose 35% or more of their income.
  • Economic Impact: Less local revenue, lost jobs, etc.

Current Legal Status

The matter is in Wisconsin’s highest courts and could reach the U.S. Supreme Court. If it prevails, it may affect other states and industries.

The Ask

As a travel advisor and concerned citizen, you are urged to:

  • Contact your state legislators (Representative, Senator, Governor).
  • Encourage them to oppose or amend Wisconsin’s taxation scheme.
  • Explain how double taxation threatens your business and Wisconsin’s economy.
