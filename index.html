Understanding the Legal / Taxation Issue

Overview of the Concern:

  • Out-of-state partners provide essential support services for Wisconsin-based travel advisors.
  • Wisconsin wants to tax 100% of the gross commissions earned by these out-of-state partners.
  • Meanwhile, travel advisors in Wisconsin already pay personal income tax on the same net commissions.

Because of this, the same commission is effectively taxed twice: once through the out-of-state partner’s gross payment and again when Wisconsin advisors pay income tax.

Why is This Potentially Problematic?

  • Commerce Clause Concerns: States usually cannot unduly burden interstate commerce.
  • Double Taxation: Advisors can lose 35% or more of their income.
  • Economic Impact: Reduced income for Wisconsin-based businesses, job losses, etc.

Current Legal Status

The matter is in Wisconsin’s highest courts and could reach the U.S. Supreme Court. If this taxation approach prevails, it may affect other states and industries too.

The Ask

As a Wisconsin travel advisor and concerned citizen, you are urged to:

  • Contact your Wisconsin legislators (Representative, Senator, Governor).
  • Encourage them to oppose or amend this double taxation approach.
  • Explain how double taxation threatens your business and Wisconsin’s economy.
